# Japan

Japanese dragon lore intertwines indigenous kami beliefs with imported motifs from neighboring [China](../China/README.md) and [Korea](../Korea/README.md). Chronicles such as the *Kojiki* and *Nihon Shoki* recount heroes confronting the eight-headed Yamata no Orochi and describe RyÅ«jin, the sea deity who commands tides and grants magical jewels.^[1^][2^] These serpentine guardians of rain and the imperial regalia embody the archipelago's maritime focus while echoing continental cosmology.^[3^]
